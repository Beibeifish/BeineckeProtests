Monday, April 21

Scrolly-telling the arrests and movement of the protests.

It became clear six hours later that arrests were imminent, as more police arrived and blocked off the plaza with caution tape.

Police began arrests just before 7am. Organizers quickly posted online, asking for supporters to return en masse, but those who arrived in time were quickly overwhelmed by police.

In less than an hour, police arrested 60 people, 47 of them Yale students, and led them through the Schwarzman Center to shuttles waiting on the intersection of College and Grove Streets.

Those who had left the plaza upon police warning began gathering at the intersection, shouting and singing in support of those arrested.

In the next few hours, more and more students arrived to occupy the streetscape. Police blocked traffic along College and Grove but issued a warning that protestors would be required to disperse by 5:00pm.

Protestors abided and moved the occupation to Cross Campus. Some students relocated a seder event to the area TKTK. For now, loud demonstrations have mostly subsided, with a smaller and quieter gathering remaining on the green Tuesday evening.

The protests remained peaceful throughout the day and night. On Sunday afternoon, the 14 Yale students in Hunger Strikers For Palestine announced that they were ending their action after eight days. At around 4:30 p.m., University President Peter Salovey broke his silence on the protests in an email to the Yale community, writing that the university would pursue disciplinary action “according to its policies.”

For now, all is calm on the plaza, but organizers have posted on their Instagram, Occupy Beinecke, asking supporters to show up to the plaza early in the morning because they expect arrests.
